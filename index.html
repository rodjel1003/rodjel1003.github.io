About Me
  • Who Am I?
    I was born in Bicol, Philippines, but I now currently live in Metro Manila.
    I am science and math genius since I was a kid.
    Believing that I will have a better life if I take up Nursing, I studied in Bicol University and finished with BS in Nursing Degree in 2009, and passed both Midwifery and Nursing Licensure Examination.
    I am person discovered his passion late in his life.
    I am a nurse turned software engineer.
    Other people may not believe that I have the skills, but I've always proved them wrong.
    One of my trainers in bootcamp asked me what I did to prepare and make sure I can cope up with my colleagues. Then, I graduated as Top 1 of the bootcamp.
    After an interview, the client decided I might have difficulty performing the job. After a 1 week trial, they ended up renewing the contract with me playing a vital role in the development of the application.
    I want to be better with my craft than yesterday, and one day become one of the best software engineers in the world.
    To be able to achieve my dreams and to accelerate my learning, I need to be surrounded by thebest people.
    Industry is moving so fast, and new technologies emerge every month. Ability to learn new skills is more important than the current skill set.
    I am not just a software engineer with 3 years of experience. I also have 4 years of experience in Customer Service/Health Insurance Industry. I worked as Customer Service Representative with clients in the US. I also managed Annual Enrolments of different clients. With my solid soft skills and passion for learning, my transition to software engineering was a second nature to me.
    It's better to be prepared and not have an opportunity, than have an opportunity and not prepared.
    I've been an intelligent person since I was a kid, but taking up nursing, something that is not my passion, made one of my biggest realizations in life. It's not just intelligence, but passion that makes one the best in his craft.
    I believe that the best way to build my future is predicting it, and guiding my way with the predictions is the key.
